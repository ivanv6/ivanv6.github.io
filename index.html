Biography

I’ve always had a special passion for computer science, software and tech- nology. From the first time I turned on a computer, I felt a particular passion for this field, developed over the years, focusing first on hardware knowledge and further engaging in software knowledge. So, I decided to make my school development on technology by attending an industrial and technical institute on computer science that provided me the fundamentals of the main disciplines used in this interesting field. Accomplished this course, I have decided to at- tend the Computer Engineering department at the DIBRIS department of the University of Genoa, in Italy. During my studies, I decided to learn new funda- mentals combined with the knowledge learned at the university by developing different kinds of personal projects, maintaining a connection between theoret- ical and practical knowledge. I have developed projects of different use and implementation, such as some websites or applications used in home automation. I privately gave computer notions, both in programming and in other disciplines such as communications networks or electronics bases, to several high school or university students.

Computer Engineer, currently research fellow in the Network Security Group of National Research Council (IEIIT) and Ph.D student in Computer Science at University of Genoa. In 2017 he obtained his MSc Degree Cum Laude in Computer Engineering from the University of Genoa, Italy, with a thesis entitled "Study of security issues on ZigBee networks in reference to the Internet of Things phenomenon". For this work, he received a degree award for the "Best project 2017" for IoT networks implementation offered by the multinational company ABB. With more then 20 certifications on cyber-security aspects and approaches, I'm always interested in learning new things and deepening the knowledge already acquired, for this reason I continue my studies through certifications in my time.

List of Projects

Advanced Networked Agents for Security and Trust Assessment in CPS / IOT Architectures (ANASTACIA)
Call H2020 (call Digital Security). The main objective of the ANASTACIA project is to address cyber-security concerns by researching, developing and demonstrating a holistic solution enabling trust and security by-design for Cyber Physical Systems (CPS) based on IoT and Cloud architectures.

Integrated Framework for Predictive and Collaborative Security of Financial Infrastructures (FINSEC)
Winner of the call H2020 (call ICT). FINSEC will provide a mature implementation of the reference architecture, based on the enhancement and integration of novel solutions of the partners which will be bundled in a toolbox. The reference architecture implementation and the toolbox will be validated through realistic pilots involving stakeholders in the identification, assessment and mitigation of threats. The five pilots involve high-impact scenarios including SWIFT network protection, buildings and ATM networks security, peer-to-peer payments network protection, risk assessment for insurance purposes and securing financial SMEs.

My HealthMy Data (MHMD)
Winner of the H2020 call (ICT call). MHMD is a Horizon 2020 Research and Innovation Action which aims at fundamentally changing the way sensitive data are shared. MHMD is poised to be the first open biomedical information network centred on the connection between organizations and individuals, encouraging hospitals to start making anonymised data available for open research, while prompting citizens to become the ultimate owners and controllers of their health data.

Maturing and Extending the FREETOOL Tool development Initiative (FREETOOL)
